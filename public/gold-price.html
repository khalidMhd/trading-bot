What moves the gold price?

Gold does not move at random. A handful of powerful forces push it up and down, and understanding them helps you make sense of the number you see above:

  • The US dollar (DXY). Gold is priced in dollars, so a stronger dollar usually makes gold cheaper and a weaker dollar lifts it. This inverse relationship is one of the most reliable in all of trading.
  • Interest rates & the Fed. Gold pays no interest, so when rates rise, holding gold becomes relatively less attractive and prices often fall. Dovish central-bank signals tend to lift gold.
  • Inflation. Gold is a classic inflation hedge. Rising inflation expectations often support higher gold prices — though the reaction depends on how markets expect the Fed to respond.
  • Geopolitical risk. In times of war, crisis, or uncertainty, investors buy gold as a safe haven, pushing prices up.
  • Supply & demand. Central-bank buying, jewellery demand, and mining supply all shape the longer-term trend.
